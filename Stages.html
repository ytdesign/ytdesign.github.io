The Stages

Featured in Home, Architecture, Agency

 

An interactive residential apartment complex designed with the concept of transitions and the idea that your home can adjust itself to your way of living. The Stages explores transitions not just as a means to an end, but as an experience in and of itself.

Prompt & Process

Design an interactive living space located in the heart of Buckhead, GA. A cross disciplinary project with Industrial Design and Architecture to design a cohesive, adaptive apartment. This project started by defining our concept of transitions and the idea that your home can adjust itself to your way of living. We then explored transitions not just as a means to an end, but as an experience in and of itself.

Unfortunately, the later portion of this project was moved to remote due to COVID-19 restrictions which affected our access to tools for physically prototype a to-scale model.

 

 

Concept

Mindset Transitions

We believe that during everyday life, we transition between three broad states of mind â€“ private, preparation, and presentation. From waking up in private to preparing oneself for the day to presenting ourselves to the world, each state of mind entails different needs and activities. We designed the apartment to facilitate the transition between these three states.

Transitions: An experience in and of itself

In addition to the changes in the state of your mind throughout the day, we looked at each transition between spaces as not just a means to an end, but a full experience too.

Site Plan

The apartment complex is located in the heart of Buckhead where residential living meets city life. This location gives residents the best of both worlds allowing a quiet escape, but also close enough to enjoy the shops and restaurants that a big city offers.

Tower

The apartments are stacked with their main axis running in the east-west direction. Each apartment benefits from both eastern and western light even as the primary exposure alternates by floor.

Sections

The architecture of the apartment allows one to have expansive views of both the east and west in order to enjoy the sunrise and sunset from one of the two extra large balconies. The architecture also allows the resident to create spaces of privacy as well as large spaces of presentation, so that one can enjoy a peaceful night alone or host a large party.

Floor Plan

Each apartment has been designed to create maximum views of the outdoors, as well as create a flow through the separate spaces of privacy, preparation, and presentation. Window walls on opposing sides of the apartment allows for crossbreeze through the apartment when opened.

Scenarios

The dynamic design of the apartment allows for a wide of range of experiences within the living space, adapting to the external environment to facilitate any activities that go on within the building.

Window System

The windows serve as the main source of protection against the elements, comprising a series of rotating glass panels that can be arranged and dimmed according to different presets. Utilizing a rotational motion, the glass panels can be opened for airflow or be cleared to the side and provide and unobstructed view out.

Shading System

The shading system is composed of Individual panels of different length that help facilitate shading and air flow. These panels follow a rotational motion which creates the transitional effect of depth. The shading system is composed of Individual panels that help facilitate shading and air flow to create a livable balcony space. These panels follow a rotational motion which creates the transitional effect of depth and can also be fully cleared to the side

Shading Mechanism

Each panel is threaded by a central pole with rotary actuators embedded within each joint. The arms differ in length ranging from 1' to 4' but are consistently 1" thick to frame the mesh that is slid in.

Shading Material

The stainless steel LPZ-62 wire mesh panels create a semi-transparent skin for this space to remain enclosed while allowing it to ventilate. The shape and texture of the crimped woven wire mesh provides unique ever-changing reflective beauty as the sun angle changes throughout the day.

Control Interface

The control panel gives users full control over most technical aspects of the house, including the window wall, shading system, air flow, temperature and acoustics. Users may tailor their internal experience to suit their needs.

Residence

The inhabitants enter on the upper level of the great space, which connects to the kitchen, dining room, and the upper balcony. They can either descend through the successive levels of the great open space towards the lower balcony, or walk up to the bedroom floor for enhanced privacy.

Axon

While differentiated by level changes, the great space is unified by a transverse spine incorporating built-in furniture and a countertop linking the kitchen to the seating area at the intermediate level. The shelves and nooks incorporated into the spine serve the adjoining spaces while also helping to declutter them.

Living Room

The living room is composed of two stepped platforms from Level 2 to Level 1, gradually transitioning users from the preparation area in the kitchen to the presentation area in the main living space.

Office

At the lowest level, a quiet working space is provided behind the spine, with direct links to the lower balcony. The central spine divides the office space from the living space, but also serves as a point of interaction between the two sections.

Kitchen

The kitchen is designed to face the window wall to enjoy the outdoor views while cleaning dishes.The kitchen connects into the spine and creates a relationship with the living room through a dip in the furniture spine.

Bedrooms

The bedrooms are located on the upper most level and creates a quiet oasis for times of privacy. They are also fitted with a private balcony for users to experience the external environment as desired.
