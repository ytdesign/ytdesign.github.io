Context

Shiseido x Changi Airport

The Jewel Changi Airport located in Singapore combines access to flight with a world-class shopping center and inspiring moments such as the world's tallest indoor waterfall, the Rain Vortex, which is surrounded by the Shiseido SENSE Forest.


Shiseido hopes to create an experience specific to highlight the Ultimune product on two mirrored locations on the second level of the shopping center section.

Shiseido

Luxury Cosmetic Company

Shiseido is a Japanese multinational personal care company that includes skin care, cosmetics, fragrance, and more. . Founded in 1872, it is one of the oldest cosmetics companies in the world.

Ultimune

“Ultimune is a product that embodies Shiseido skincare. It strengthens the skin’s natural powers of defense, enabling it to build its own beauty. It enhances the benefits of skincare routines that follow.” - Official Shiseido Website

Concept

Sense of Presence

Beauty's intangibility lead us to True Beauty is not just the essence of what you are but also the presence of which you exude so our concept - sense of presence - encapsulates the sheer essence of Shiseido’s beauty that is awakened by extending its radiance and aura into the surroundings

 

Concept Developement

Medium

In line with Shiseido’s love for nature, we awaken beauty through the mimicry Bamboo forests and fields which led us to our medium of choice: Rods.

Benchmarking

We were intrigued by how this simple typology of an extruded hole produce different phenomena experiences and created volume by varying its colour, density, and spatial sensation.

Senses

To create an emcompassing experience, we strove to embody all five sense and create zones of concentration.

Scent Technology

We discovered technology to infuse scent into mist within a bubble that could spread once the bubble popped as seen in COS x Swine Studio New Spring to continue the intangibility of presence.

Exploration

To decide on the path of the forest, we explored many different configerations and different densities of the rods to ensure the presence was there, but not overwhelming at the same time.

Focus

We wanted to focus on three things: Enclosed Space to create clear boundaries just for Shiseido, a sense of Mystery created by the 1.2 meter height that encourages guests to peer in, and finally a precious center to highlight the Ultimune bottle and inner beauty.

Zoning

By gathering the experiences into zones, the user can approach from any direction and visit each zone in any order.

App Developement

To connect the users to the Ultimune product and create an augmented experience, we developed an app as a guide to the space with digital interactive elements.

Details

System Parts

CMF

Reflective material such as chrome and glossy finishes were selected to create more depth and form while highlighting Shiseido’s brand colors. For cost restraints, PVC can be used to substitute acrylic, however color selection would not be as accurate.

Assembly

Designed to be assembled overnight, each piece is sectioned off, and rods are to be pressure fitted.

Technology

Wires for the lights are threaded through hollow tubes and extend to the ground. Motion sensors are concealed below and will guide the bubble producing frequency. As more guests approach, more bubbles will be produced, and when there are less guests, the bubbles and lights will slow down to save energy. The bubble solution and mist solution are hidden within the space created by the topographical form, and travel through tubes up the pipes. Nozzles are placed at the top of selected bubble rods to produce the bubbles.
