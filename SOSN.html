Boutique Wedding Show

Timeline
Summer 2019 - 11 Weeks

Designing to create and build relationships between a venue, clients, and vendors.

 

Through preliminary market research, the wedding show was positioned and branded to gather vendors, potential clients, and designed to help brides and grooms find exactly what make their wedding not just perfect, but perfect for them!

 

View Website

 

 

Context

The Historic Academy of Medicine

One of Atlanta’s premier venues for weddings, receptions, conferences, and other special events, The Historic Academy of Medicine offers an unparalleled opportunity to host functions in one of the most beautiful and historic settings in the South.

The Show

With the goal of sharing the newly renovated building with the community, the first Something Old, Something New Wedding Show was held in 2012. Over the years, we’ve enjoyed reconnecting with the community, meeting our neighbors, supporting our Vendors, and helping our bride and grooms. Due to the success of previous years, we are excited to present this year’s Boutique Wedding Show where you can find everything you need to create a wedding with your own unique touch: Something Special, Something You!

Stakeholders

Positioning: Boutique

The size of the venue is smaller than the large conventions wedding shows can be. Combined with the historic feeling of the Historic Academy of Medicine, we decided to position the show as boutique and unique with the title:

Something Old, Something New, Something Special, Something You.

Show Concept & Design

Glam & Grace

Researching into trends of the year combined with the venue positioning, Glam & Grace combines the grandeur of a wedding with an elegant touch.

Vendors

Vendors were selected for their compatibility with the show image and categories were limited to ensure each vendor had a chance to shine while still providing options for the guests. Out of 204 vendors contacted, 38 supported the show.

Marketing

Website

I created a website on WIX as the touchpoint for both guests and vendors. It was updated regularly with vendors who supported the show.

View Website

Invitations

Printed invitations were given to vendors to share with their clients.

Posters & Listings

Social Media

As a part of show and vendor marketing, I ran a social media campaign on Facebook and Instagram over 4 weeks. Each post started with a show branded vendor logo followed by the show and then vendor highlight pictures.

Day Of

Registration

Custom sticker badges were designed and printed for guests and helped vendors tailor their conversations.

Final Guest Count & Feedback

The final RSVP guest count exceeded the goal of 200 guest, and over 90% of guests were wedding related. Some suggestions for next year were more dresses, and while customer satisfaction averaged 4.32 out of 5, vendors would still like to see more guests.
