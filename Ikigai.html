Net Bag

Selected for NUS Gradbook 2020

 

Exploring the philosophy of Ikigai, what if we could inspire utilitarian craftsmanship through simple weaving with cheap materials like plastic bags? Through flow in making, join our program to create human bonds in the form of shibui aesthetics and further the maker’s movement together.

Prompt & Process

Design with the philosopy of Ikigai in mind. Through many readings, reflections, explorations, and thoughts, our team strove to create interest and provoke thought and reflection through a non-linear path.

 

 

Ikigai

Ikigai is a Japanese philosophy that intersects what you love, what you're good at, what you can be paid for, and what the world needs.

The Unknown Craftsman

Mingei

Japanese philosopher Soetsu Yanagi first expresses this through Chinese ceramics from the Song dynasty. He called this “Mingei”, or the handcrafted art of ordinary people. In the world of mingei, the aim is to produce things through the medium of man.Making is beautiful, in and of itself.

Flow

As one indulges in the simple work of making, one is greeted by flow. Just think back to those moments where you were completely engrossed in your work, and didn’t even notice how the hours passed. In this state of flow, there is no hesitation, no ambition. There is no ‘I’, only the ‘making’.

Exploration

Concept Exploration

Each of us selected moments of interest to us and played with potential translations into a bag.

Concept Selection: Nets

We found the idea of throwing everything you want into a bag that would filter through and uncover the essentials to be really interesting. Inspired by a nomadic lifestyle in today's world where there are so many options and choices, we continued to develope our concept into a net where only the essentials can be kept as a reminder of what is really important: the intangible.

Material Exploration

Another part of Ikigai is also the importance of sustainiblity, so we explored different materials and realized we could repurpose plastic bags into a different form.

Final Design

Introduction

Symbol

We chose to create a programme that merges two things: flow and people. Flow is brought forth through the making process of repetitive looping that encourages users to be fully immersed in making which is symbolized here in a loop, and people which is represented in its chinese character form, 'ren'.

Maker's Tutorial

Sustainability Program

We see this program being implemented at multiple levels.

First, individuals start by learning the craft. Schools could leverage on this programme for their upcycling initiatives. Community centres could hold workshops to teach the basic techniques.

At the next level, community kicks in. Bag weaving could be a great bonding activity for children with their parents or grandparents. It could be a great engagement activity with elderly in nursing homes as well.

At some point, maker enthusiasts may surface with a high level of mastery. From them we may see new bag designs that may make their way into maker’s fairs, further inspiring the maker’s movement.
